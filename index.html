The Origin Story

A wasted year, a trip to Walgreens and an envelope in a glovebox.

25 years.
Hundreds of players.
Thousands of practices.
Millions of minutes.

Jerritt Elliott is widely known as one of the best college volleyball coaches of all-time. Like all great coaches, he strives for efficiency and despises wasted motion and wasted time.

While running his practices, Jerritt noticed something. His players often had to stop to adjust their hair. When he calculated the time wasted, it added up to over a year of lost time.

It frustrated Jerritt so much that after one practice, he drove straight to Walgreens and bought a bag of hair ties. He needed to understand why they didn't work.

The Origin Story

At Walgreens, he bought a bag of 30 'drugstore' hair ties. The hair tie all his players used. The one that didn't work.

Like a mad scientist, he stretched them, cut them, pulled them apart and put them back together.

Like any great inventor, Jerritt envisioned a better hair tie...how it would work, how it would feel and what it would look like.

After many months of tinkering and trials, he had developed the first version of TIY. He lent one to his girlfriend at the time, pro volleyball player Andrea Nucete. She put the envelope of hair ties in the glovebox of her car.

Even she was skeptical. "Why would I tie my own hair tie? What is the benefit? I don't trust the bald guy."

The Origin Story

Until she was driving to a tournament in Houston and both of her 'drugstore' hair ties snapped.

She remembered the envelope in her glovebox that contained Jerritt's invention.

Out of necessity, she tried it. And immediately, she "got it."

It wasn't just better, it was beautiful. Function and fashion. It kept her hair in place during a tough match and the colors happened to match her outfit.

Today, Jerritt and Andrea Nucete-Elliott are married and Andrea is the CEO of TIY.

The company has grown to sell millions of hair ties and is proudly worn by top Olympic athletes and everyday women.
